# Source Map — Maximus People Finder

Reference table for Step 3 (Multi-Channel Search) and Step 4 (Enrichment). Use this to decide which channels are worth running for a given brief, and how much to trust what each one returns.

| Source | Best for | Access / cost / rate limits | Verification difficulty |
|---|---|---|---|
| **LinkedIn-equivalent people search** (`search_vertical(vertical="people")`) | Current role/title/company/location discovery; the default first channel for almost any brief. | Free via `search_vertical`; results are search-engine-mediated, not a raw scrape — don't construct profile URLs by hand. | Medium — titles can be stale if the underlying page hasn't been re-indexed recently; cross-check recency against a second source for decision-maker or current-role briefs. |
| **X/Twitter** | Recent activity, topic engagement, self-declared current role/affiliation in bio, journalist beat signals. | Free via `search_web`/`fetch_url` on public posts; no login-gated scraping. | Medium-high for identity (bios can be outdated or joke-y); good for recency signal once identity is confirmed elsewhere. |
| **GitHub** | Technical experts: repo ownership, org membership, commit activity, README/profile bios, OSS maintainer status. | Free, public API/pages via `fetch_url`; rate limits apply to unauthenticated API calls, so prefer profile/repo pages over bulk API pulls. | Low-medium — commit history and org membership are hard to fake, making this one of the stronger corroborating channels for technical candidates. |
| **Company sites (About/Team/Leadership/Careers)** | Most authoritative source for *current* role and title at a specific company; org-chart mapping for decision-maker briefs. | Free via `fetch_url`; some sites gate team pages behind JS rendering — note if a page appears empty and try a cached/alternate source. | Low — this is closest to primary-source for current employment, but can lag actual personnel changes by weeks. |
| **News and press** (`search_web`) | Funding announcements, executive moves, quote attribution, general recency signal. | Free; use `recency_filter` to bias toward current coverage. | Medium — press often repeats a PR-supplied title that may already be stale by publication; treat as one signal, not the final word on current role. |
| **Academic papers** (`search_vertical(vertical="academic")`) | Authorship, institutional affiliation, citation trails for subject-matter-expert and advisory-board briefs. | Free; returns title/authors/date/DOI. | Low for identity (affiliations are usually explicit and dated), but affiliation may be 1-2 years stale by publication lag — cross-check current employer separately. |
| **Podcasts and conference speaker lists** | Topic-authority signals, guest/speaker discovery for event and podcast-booking briefs. | Free via `fetch_url` on agenda/guest pages; coverage varies a lot by how well an event publishes its lineup. | Medium — a past speaking slot confirms topic authority at that point in time, not necessarily current role; re-verify current affiliation separately. |
| **SEC EDGAR filings** | Executive officers and board members at public companies (proxy statements, 8-Ks for leadership changes). | Free, public, no rate limit of consequence via `fetch_url`/EDGAR full-text search. | Low — this is a primary legal document; among the most reliable sources available for public-company leadership and board rosters. |
| **Crunchbase / PitchBook** | Investor identification, fund details, portfolio mapping, company funding history. | Public pages accessible via `fetch_url`; full data often paywalled. Check `list_external_tools` first for a connected data source before relying on public-page scraping. | Medium — investor/fund data is generally well-maintained, but "led by" attribution on aggregator sites sometimes differs from the actual press release; cross-check against the original funding announcement. |
| **Newsletter / Substack search** | Journalist and independent-analyst discovery, especially for beats where independent writers matter as much as staff reporters. | Free via `search_web`; some newsletters are subscriber-gated for full content but bylines/summaries are usually public. | Medium — an active newsletter today may go dormant; check publish date of the most recent issue before ranking a writer as active. |
| **Alumni pages / company alumni networks** | "Ex-Company X, now at Company Y" briefs. | Free where such pages exist (not all companies publish them); otherwise reconstruct via people-vertical search filtered on past+current employer. | Medium-high — common-name collisions are a real risk here; require a second disambiguating detail (team, tenure era, location) before merging. |
| **Press releases (company-issued)** | Executive appointments, board additions, partnership announcements — a primary source for the *fact* of a role, even if promotional in tone. | Free via `fetch_url`. | Low for the basic fact (title, date, company); treat superlative claims in the same release with more skepticism. |

## General notes

- **No channel is sufficient alone.** Even a strong primary source (SEC filing, company site) benefits from a second confirming signal when the brief is high-stakes (e.g. outreach that references specific, potentially sensitive details).
- **Recency beats authority when they conflict.** A six-month-old primary-source title can be wrong if a press release from last week announced a move. Prefer the most recent dated source when two disagree.
- **Rate limits and access:** most of these channels are reachable through `search_web`, `search_vertical`, and `fetch_url` without special access. Where a paid/connector-gated source exists (Crunchbase, PitchBook, LinkedIn Sales Navigator-equivalent), check `list_external_tools` before relying on public-page workarounds — a connected source is both more reliable and more respectful of the platform's terms.
- **Never construct profile URLs by pattern-guessing.** Every URL in a delivered candidate row must be one actually returned by a search tool or fetched directly — not assembled from a guessed username scheme.
- **PII boundary applies to every source in this table.** None of these channels justify pulling a personal phone number or home address. Contact hints stay limited to what the person or their employer has made public (company email pattern, public contact form, public social handle).

# CTA Managed-Account Example

## Search brief

Find family offices, investment entities, registered or exempt allocators, CTAs investing in other CTAs, and individual accredited-investor prospects potentially suitable for a separately managed futures account with a $100,000 minimum.

## Positive signals

- Explicit managed-futures, alternatives, macro, futures, commodities, trend-following, or diversifier interest.
- Existing allocations to CTAs, CPOs, funds of funds, multi-manager futures products, or separately managed accounts.
- Documented liquidity or investment capacity materially above the minimum.
- Recent business sale, insider stock sale, fund distribution, or other verified liquidity event.
- Publicly stated willingness to evaluate emerging managers.
- Professional background in futures, derivatives, family-office investing, hedge funds, or alternatives.

## Specialized discovery paths

- Insolvent CTA/CPO matters: identify public bankruptcy or receivership records that name LPs, creditors, account holders, feeder funds, introducing brokers, or counterparties. Check whether disclosure is lawful and current; never treat loss history as a reason to exploit vulnerability.
- CTA allocators: identify CTAs/CPOs operating multi-advisor pools, manager-of-managers products, external allocations, seed programs, or strategic sub-advisory relationships.
- Public forum participants: identify users who publicly discuss substantial futures accounts or manager selection. Keep pseudonymous unless independently verified; do not expose private identity.
- High-value property sale: use a confirmed sale of $5 million or more only as a weak liquidity signal. Suppress residential address and require another fit signal.
- SEC Form 4 sale: calculate potential gross sales of $5 million or more from open-market sale transactions, review footnotes and ownership type, and require another fit signal.

## Exclusions and review

Exclude or flag:
- sanctioned or prohibited parties
- unresolved fraud findings or material disciplinary history
- evidence of financial distress that makes outreach inappropriate
- persons whose only evidence is a home sale, inheritance speculation, or anonymous boasting
- retail consumers without a reasonable basis for program fit
- anyone marked do-not-contact

## Outreach gate

Discovery does not authorize solicitation. Before outreach, require identity verification, lawful contact basis, registration review, offering/disclosure review, suitability process, and compliance approval. Accredited-investor status and CTA account suitability are separate determinations.

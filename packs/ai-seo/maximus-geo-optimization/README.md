# maximus-geo-optimization

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO): the discipline of getting a brand, product, or piece of expertise surfaced and cited inside answers generated by Perplexity, Google AI Overviews (SGE), ChatGPT search, You.com, Bing Copilot, and Claude with web access.

## What this skill covers

GEO sits next to AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) but solves a different problem. AEO is about being extractable — formatted so a model *can* quote you cleanly. GEO is about being surfaced in the first place — trusted, crawlable, and mentioned widely enough that a generative model *chooses* you as a source among thousands of candidates.

This skill package includes:

- **SKILL.md** — the core reference: the 5 GEO levers, prompt mining workflow, third-party mention strategy, robots.txt guidance for LLM crawlers, content freshness signals, and anti-patterns.
- **HOWTO.md** — six practical recipes covering prompt mining, robots.txt audits, third-party mention planning, writing for listicle inclusion, content refresh cycles, and handoff to visibility tracking.
- **examples/prompt-mining-trace.md** — a full worked example of mining and clustering 20 real buyer prompts for a SaaS category, mapped to a content plan.
- **examples/robots-config-trace.md** — a full worked example of auditing an existing robots.txt and producing a corrected, GEO-aware version with rationale for each crawler decision.
- **references/llm-crawler-directory.md** — a reference table of the major LLM/AI crawlers (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, Google-Extended, CCBot, and others), what each is for, and how to allow or block them.

## Who this is for

Anyone doing SEO, content, or growth work who needs their brand to show up when buyers ask an LLM instead of typing into Google — marketers, content strategists, technical SEOs, and founders managing their own organic presence.
